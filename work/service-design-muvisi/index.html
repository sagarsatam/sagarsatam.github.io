muvisi.

A mobility service that streamlines parking across the entire ecosystem of drivers, operators and municipality.


For drivers it provides an end-to-end solution through reliable parking availability data; which makes it stress-free and convenient to find & navigate to on-street parking spots, track sessions and pay seamlessly for actual parked time.


Whilst assisting parking operators & cities to manage their assets more effectively.


*This project was carried out while working at the design agency, THINGS, in Milan.

Get in touch with me for more details related to this project; such as my role, the problems i faced, the processes i used and how i addressed the challenges.


Things.is a design and innovation agency for the Internet of Things. We create products and services with their own experiential ecosystems outlined by multi-channel touch points. We envision products and services in the IoT era through a human-centred approach. www.things.is

Context of the project

This project is made up of 2 phases.

In phase 1 of the project, our scope was to consider only the 'on-street' (Blue lines) parking scenario for few selected cities in Italy.

In phase 2, we plan to evolve the service to also include the use case of 'off-street' parking, while also scaling the service to even more cities in Italy.

Project overview

PROJECT TYPE: An urban mobility service that assists users to find parking availability.
TEAM MEMBERS: Design team of THINGS, Milan
MY ROLE: Benchmarking competitors, Blue-sky research to gather inspiration from other industries, preparing for on-field interviews, carrying out service safaris, user-behaviour observation and interviews, research analysis, brand strategy, experience mapping front & back office, wireframing digital product, visual design and prototyping of digital touchpoints.

the challenge

Parking is one of the most difficult and dysfunctional parts of Italian city life.

We aimed to solve these parking problems by creating a fully digitalized parking ecosystem that address all sides of the market; drivers (B2C), private parking operators (B2B) and city municipalities as a white-label product (B2G).

02

the approach





Understanding the current parking landscape

We initiated the project by learning about different on-street parking typologies, their rules & regulations set by city municipality.

The citys parking consists of white lines (free parking), yellow lines (resident parking) and blue lines (paid parking); the later is the only parking typology we considered for phase 1 of the project (scope).

This followed a benchmarking study to analyse the existing competitors in Italy, Europe and beyond.

The competitors we identified were mapped across 2 independent variables (payment method & parking space visibility) to understand their positioning and how we could strategically place ourselves in the market.

Getting out onto the streets

It was vital to step into the shoes of the drivers and experience first hand what it is like to drive and find parking in the city.

We carefully selected contexts/scenarios for its unique characteristics, which would give us as many insights as possible:

  1. Finding parking in known vs. unknown location
  2. Finding parking near destination vs. pre-planning route
  3. Finding parking at different times of the day
  4. Paying using sms vs. parking meter

We also tried the various apps that we discovered during benchmarking study.

We learned that finding parking is a stressful situation due to: low availability, uncertainty of spots and time spent to find parking, pressure of surrounding cars & inconvenient payment.

Contextual interviews and behavioural observations

Next, we prepared for field research by outlining the goals of user-observation & interview questions.

Our goal was to meet with users to learn about their habits & behaviours, what drives their current choices when it comes to going from point A to B in the city, what frustrates them, what makes them happy and what would make them change their routine.

Our questions focused on the entire journey of the user.

  1. Before: planning their upcoming journey, getting in their car, driving to their destination.

  2. During: Arriving near their destination, parking their car.

  3. After: doing their thing, returning to their car, paying for their session, driving off.

"Key information is not clear such as max. allowed time to park, parking rules, exceptions and city restrictions."

"Using the parking meter needs coins/cash. Plus I need to guess how much time I will stay there... and If I have to extend I need to come back to my car & parking meter"

Research analysis & insight generation

All our research findings was put together for us to begin our analysis. We started by making clusters so that we could highlight patterns and key elements.

When users are in a particular context/scenario, they have certain jobs they have to do. The way people do these jobs (behaviour) and the needs of the people depend on their mindset and personalities.

Based on our interviews and observations we identified 3 different personality traits - mindsets

Our research also allowed us to identify the different scenarios, contexts and Jobs-to-be-done that users have.

  1. Using an app to find parking vs. not using any app.
  2. Going to the same location regularly.
  3. Going to a location casually.
  4. Go to new locations or areas they are not familiar with.
  5. Parking for short vs. long period of time.

We then extracted insights and identified opportunity areas for our solution. Insights were converted to How Might We statements.

We found out that most car journeys in cities involve parking pain on daily basis; from uncertainty (parking availability, prices, restrictions) to inconvenience (full car parks, time spent to find parking, slow payments, pressure of surrounding cars & unfair fines).

UX Stratergy

Formulating a UX strategy involved laying down the guiding principles of our brand & its positioning, our challenges & desired outcomes, key values, contexts & users we would focus on, short-long term goals, KPIs, and service evolution strategy.

Experience design of mobile app

The identified strategy, research insights and opportunities were used to outline the overall experience we wanted to offer to users and the back-office stakeholders.

Consolidation of various functionalities and its prioritisation based on user needs, business goals & project timeline was carried out.

This work formed the basis for mapping the end-to-end service; that included both online & offline user experience and its corresponding technical requirement in the back-end.

We faced many challenges from technical constraints, business requirements to city regulations and after a few discussions with clients and iterations we started making wireframes of the mobile app.

Low-fidelity wireframing of key interactions and functionalities allowed us to quickly test various alternatives and consolidate the experience.

Experience design of back-office system

Based on the functionalities & user flow of the front-office, the back-office features were defined.

The back-office was to designed to be an operational and analytical dashboard that would give insights to respective stakeholders so as to take data informed decisions on how to further optimise the mobility, in terms of traffic and vehicle flow in the city.

A hierarchy system depending on their roles, skills, level of visibility and control in the back-office system was created.

For each stakeholder we understood their access restrictions, jobs-to-do, goals and needs while also taking into account their technical knowledge and familiarity with the parking system.

This allowed us to map out the touchpoint flows for various use-cases. We then created wireframes of various sections and prioritised the layout according to information hierarchy based on each stakeholder's needs.

Brand personality & visual language

Next, we defined our strategy to ensure that the business vision, user needs, and technical capabilities are aligned to our priorities & focus on delivering the best possible experience in the areas that matter the most to our users.

We came up a visual concept that was calm & minimal while at the same time stood out for its futuristic elements; this also tied-in perfectly with the overall values of our service.

the outcome

We have designed a suite of fully integrated and comprehensive parking management solution for Drivers, City Municipalities (as white-label) and Private operators to manage their parking assets.

Muvisi mobile app helps users simplify their parking experience by leveraging on various data sources and IoT technology.

The back-office system & dashboard provides a macro-view of the entire parking ecosystem bringing insights into parking behavior so that quick data-informed decisions can be made.

Visit muvisi.app to find out more.

Pilot launch

The product was planned to be launched in the market as a pilot for 3 month in the Italian Province of Pesaro and Urbino. However, due to COVID-19 this was put on hold.

We aimed to collect feedback during the pilot study to consolidate the on-street parking service. At the same time, evolve our service offering by adding off-street parking garages to the Muvisi ecosystem.











Problems faced during the project and how they were solved

We found out from our testing that it was easy for users to do certain things on the app, only to realise in the next moment that they didnt want to make that decision or have changed their mind.

We solved this problem by first identifying points in the interaction that required users to make a conscious decision and then introduced intentional friction (animations & slide interaction) in the experience so that they would be sure of the decision they were making.

Quantitative data and field research had given us couple of important insights which proved our initial assumptions wrong.

We (design team) convinced the other teams (business, front & back-end technical) the necessity of changing some features and including new features in the service; whilst balancing the effort, complexity and cost.

We used Google APIs for our maps to call for Point of Interest (POI) information when users filter or search for a POI, where each call incurred certain cost for us. This wasnt business viable considering the use cases and behaviours users had on our app.

We solved this by making a single call where we got all the POI information, but only the filter or information that was searched by the user was shown to them. So the next time when the user searched or filtered another POI, that POI could be shown to user without making another API call
