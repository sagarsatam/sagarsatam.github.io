Service Design, User Experience, Digital UX/UI, Interaction design

electric car charging service.

An IoT service that provides an end-to-end solution for electric car owners to find, book, track & pay for their parking & charging sessions; on-street and in-facilities.


Overall, we created an attitude towards an environmentally-friendly mobility future by bringing easy & intuitive access to the charging infrastructure.


*This project was carried out while working at the design agency, THINGS, in Milan.

Context of the project

Two services for the Electric Mobility of Mercedes-Benz group were to be designed. The first service was for the Smart group and the second for EQ Electric Intelligence.

Currently, Smart car owners use a mobile app called Ready to Park, for finding parking in urban areas. We had to fully redesign the existing experience of this service and also add new features that would allow owners of Smart electric drive vehicles to charge their cars.

Project overview

PROJECT TYPE: Designing a physical-digital mobility experience that helps owners of electric cars to charge their vehicles.
TEAM MEMBERS: Design team of THINGS, Milan
MY ROLE: Conducting service safaris, mapping current & future user journey, user flows of the mobile app, wireframing, planning & conducting usability testing, carrying contextual interviews, preparing questionnaires, analysing test results, UI design, prototyping on Adobe XD.

CLIENT
01

challenge

1 - Smart group

Smart have officially announced that it will become an electric-only brand, starting from the end of 2019.

Our brief was to design a charging ecosystem that would provide a seamless end-to-end service for users to charge their Smart electric drive cars.

This will help in-turn increase the conversion rate of current internal combustion engine customers into electric drive.

Our challenge was to evolve and extend the existing Ready to Park service experience by providing charging features within it.

2 - EQ (Electric Intelligence)


With the introduction of first Mercedes-Benz Plug-In Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEV) and Battery Electric Vehicles (BEV), the creation of a wide portfolio of EQ services was necessary.

Our service was intended to be available only to PHEV/BEV vehicles owners with the aim to provide customers a complete solution for charging their PHEV/BEV Mercedes-Benz.

02

process









On-field research & service safari

The first step involved gaining an understanding on how the existing Ready to Park service worked.

In order to get a clear understanding of how the existing service worked, we went out onto the streets with the Smart Electric car and simulated various use-cases with the existing Ready to Park app.

We also observed and interviewed parking operators of these facilities to understand their current work-flow.

Knowing how the back-office management dashboard, Quisque, was used by parking operators to manage parking allowed us to understand how the service was delivered to the users. And if there were any constraints, needs, and pain points in the delivery of service to the users.

Integration with the Quisque back-office system

The service designed was integrated with the existing Quisque back-office dashboard.

Quisque is an enterprise parking management system designed to provide the most relevant information in the least obtrusive fashion.

The back office experience was also taken into consideration when designing the service so as to ensure effective service provision by parking & charging facility operators to the users.

Research analysis & UX strategy

The research findings were gathered and used to map out different phases of the existing service experience.

Mapping out the end-to-end user journey allowed us to visualise all current steps and gain insights on the critical points of the existing Ready to Park service.

From this we identified key areas of the user experience we needed to evolve & re-design in order to integrate the new charging features.

The existing experience of Ready to Park was evolved into a Ready to Park & Charge for 2 main contexts:

Charging of electric car at an automatic & manually operated garages (off-street).

Charging of electric car at on-street charging stations.

Critical points identified for evolving & re-designing the service were:

1) Searching on-street & off-street charging stations.

2) Booking charging stations for a period of time.

3) Physical & digital interaction with charging stations to start, track & end sessions.

User experience and interaction design of mobile app

Mapping the user journeys allowed us the identify the various touchpoints in this service.

  1. Human - parking operators

  2. Physical - electric car, charging cables, RFID charging card, charging stations

  3. Space - manual and automatic parking garages, on-street charging stations

  4. Digital - mobile app

The needs, requirements and usage contexts of the user while interacting with different touchpoints was analysed to ensure a seamless interconnected experience between them.

For the mobile app touchpoint, the features and the respective screens were initially designed by making low fidelity wireframes on paper of key screens of the app.

This allowed us to quickly iterate and optimise an initial version of information architecture and user flow.

Particular attention was given to the interaction between the user in the mobile app and physical-spatial context.

For e.g. we gave step by step instructions that assists users on how to efficiently interact with the physical infrastructure to start and end their charging session.

High-fidelity Wireframes of the entire app were mapped in a flow for various user scenarios.

Discussion with stakeholders and the client then allowed us to consolidate the screens and proceed to making the visual design and development of the app.

Visual design of Ready to Park & Charge

A refreshment of the old visual identity of the Ready to Park app was to be made.

Most elements had to be re-designed, except those with respect to the charging functionalities that had to be newly designed, whilst keeping the same visual language as before.

Clear icons to show the different status of the parking and charging facilities.

Visually representing the timer in a way that shows the booked period of the charging stations at a glance.

Illustrations that guide users to finding the right charging station and start, track & end session.

User validation & app usability testing

Once the new Ready to Park & Charge app was developed, we planned for testing and validation with users and parking operators.

The goal was to identify breaking points of the experience, why they were so and define insights to fix them.

Test cards were made to organise our research.

why we are doing this test?
what do we really want to know/learn from this test?
what action could we take if we knew that number or rate?
what assumptions are to be validated?

Our testing focused on key moments - booking, driving to facility, arrival & car handover, charging of car and payment.

Certain KPIs were defined so that we could measure metrics, analyse & learn from it. We also defined behaviours to observe and the key gains, pains and tasks we wanted to test.

The testing was carried out in the city of Rome, Italy; 4 scenarios and 3 variables were tested.

Different research methods and tools were used:

  1. Observing participants using the service.

  2. Interviewing users and parking operators.

  3. Evaluating users mobile phone usage through a mobile screen & voice recording app.

  4. Questionnaires for users & parking operators to rate the key steps of the experience.

Consolidation of app usability based on user testing & feedback

After the testing session, the results and feedback were reviewed to identify the critical problem areas that had to be addressed.

Insights were drawn and improvement were made to the user experience of the mobile app and other aspects of the service.

Clear and simple way to warn users on the app if the booking they want to make will expire before they reach the facility.

Blinking of the booked charging station when users' smartphone is in close proximity to it. This would make it easy to identify the charging station and park the car accordingly.

Designing a watch interface for seamless service experience

We also decided to allow users who have an Apple Watch to carry out main actions using their watch. For each phase of the experience we analysed the main interactions between the user and mobile app, and these were transferred to the watch interface.

Key watch interactions such as tracking the countdown timer of booking while on-route to the facility, and opening the gate/bar of the parking facility made the service user-friendly; considering that the user is behind the wheel in these interaction contexts.

EQ Charge mobile app - Visual design

The EQ Charge service worked in a similar way to the one of the Ready to Park & Charge. Once the Ready to Park & Charge app was completely designed, the visual language of the EQ service was made.

We came up with options for colour scheme, buttons, key interactive elements, micro animations & illustrations that reflected the brand identity of Mercedes-Benz EQ.

Testing and consolidation of the visual language of the EQ Charge mobile app

Key elements on screens were tested to evaluate how well the users understood and perceived these visual elements.

Based on the feedback, we consolidated the visual design of the mobile app.

03

outcome

Electric mobility should be easy, fast & convenient; and above all, directly available. With the Ready to Park & Charge and Mercedes EQ Charge we have designed two innovative mobility services for the urban mobility eco-system and smart city experiences.

With just a swipe on their smartphone we have made it easier than ever for electric car users to find, book, charge and pay for their parking & charging sessions; while guiding them through the whole process.

Studying users needs and the existing urban infrastructure, we designed new functionalities that fit perfectly into the existing service, thus adding value by creating a more holistic service ecosystem for Mercedes-Benz.





















Problems faced during the project and how they were solved

A major service constraint (from the parking operators & energy providers) was the different booking expiration time for parking and charging services. This resulted in subsequent complexities that had to be designed around.

We designed various interactions that enabled users to first clearly understand these rules & its implications. Secondly the experience we designed allowed them to take appropriate action easily.

Due to a mix of physical, spatial and digital actions that the user had to perform there was a high probability of creating a disjointed experience, if not designed properly.

We overcame this challenge by detailing out points in the experience where this switch between physical-spatial-digital was occurring and then designing elements that joined to create a seamless and cohesive experience.

Since we were working with parking and charging facilities that were underground, there was unreliable or a lack of wireless network connectivity.

Bluetooth connectivity was used instead to remotely open gates to the parking facilities, to unlock/lock the hatchet of charging stations and to start/end charging sessions.



Reflection - what I learnt during the project & what I could have done better.

Due to premise of this project, our design team was not involved in the re-designing of the back-office system, Quisque, that was used by the parking and charging operators.

This meant that we were not able to improve the experience of the parking operators, so that their work-flow can be in-line with the newly re-designed app for the users.

This resulted in some unmet needs of the parking operators which sometimes affected the quality of the service provided to the users, when they reached the facility and interacted with the operators.

In order to deign a service holistically it is vital to have the ability to improve the experience of all the key stakeholders.
