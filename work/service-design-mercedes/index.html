electric car charging service.

An IoT service that provides an end-to-end solution for electric car owners to find, book & pay for their parking & charging sessions, on-street and in-facilities. Overall, we created an attitude towards the future through a holistic service that brings intuitive access to the charging infrastructure.

01

challenge

Two services for the Electric Mobility of Mercedes-Benz group were to be designed. The first service was for the Smart group and the second was for EQ Electric Intelligence.

1 - New Smart Ready to Park & Charge mobile app

Smart have officially announced that it will become an electric-only brand, starting from the end of 2019. Our aim was to design a charging ecosystem with a seamless end-to-end service for users to charge their Smart electric cars. This will in-turn increase the conversion rate of current internal combustion engine customers into electric drive.

YEAR: 2018 - 2019DURATION: 4 monthsPROJECT TYPE: Designing a physical-digital mobility experience that helps owners of electric cars to charge their vehicles.TEAM MEMBERS: Design team of THINGS, MilanMY ROLE: Conducting service safaris, mapping current & future user journey, user flows of the mobile app, wireframing, planning & conducting usability testing, carrying contextual interviews, preparing questionnaires, analysing test results, UI design consultation, prototyping on Adobe XD.
CLIENT

Currently, Smart car owners use a mobile app called Ready to Park, for finding parking in urban areas. The challenge was to evolve and extend the current Ready to Park app by providing charging functionalities within it.

2 - EQ (Electric Intelligence) Charge mobile app

With the introduction of first Mercedes-Benz Plug-In Hybrid Electric Vehicles (PHEV) and Battery Electric Vehicles (BEV), the creation of a wide portfolio of EQ services was necessary. This service is intended to be available only to PHEV/BEV vehicles owners with the aim to provide customers a complete solution for charging their PHEV/BEV Mercedes-Benz, so to offer more charging possibilities and fight charging/range anxiety. The design aim for EQ was to adapt the customer experience of Smart Ready to Park & Charge into a EQ Ready to Charge with look and feel of the Mercedes-Benz EQ brand.

02

process

On-field research & service safari

The first step involved gaining an understanding on how the existing Ready to Park service worked. To do this, we experienced the service first hand by driving the Smart Electric car and simulating various use-cases & personas to find parking using the Ready to Park app.

We also observed and interviewed parking operators of these facilities to understand their current work-flow and how the back-office management dashboard, Quisque, was used by these operators to manage parking during their daily workflow.

Research analysis & UX strategy

The research findings were gathered to identify the different phases of the service experience. Mapping out the user journey allowed us to visualise the current steps and gain insights on the critical points of the Ready to Park service. From this we identified key areas of the user experience we need to evolve to integrate the new charging functionalities into the existing parking experience.

The existing customer journey of Ready to Park was evolved into a Ready to Park & Charge for 2 main contexts:

  1. Charging in one of Ready to Park automatic and manually operated garages.
  2. Charging stand alone at on-street pole stations.

An end-to-end user experience (both physical and digital) for the new Ready to Park & Charge service was mapped out ftor he main service phases:

  1. Searching for parking &/or charging stations
  2. Reserve for a period of time
  3. Accessing charging stations in off-street facilities/garages and on-street
  4. Starting, tracking & ending charging sessions with the smartphone/RFID card
  5. Paying through the mobile app
All interactions between smartphone. RFID card and charging station were to be implemented with machine-to-machine connection and had to work properly even without 3G/4G signal.

Integration with the Quisque back-end systems

The back office experience was also taken into account in order to ensure that the service provided to its users was effectively delivered by the operators of parking & charging facilities. The service designed was integrated with the existing Quisque back-office dashboard. Quisque is an enterprise parking management system designed to provide the most relevant information in the least obtrusive fashion.

User experience, interaction and visual design of the mobile app

From the above user flow the different touchpoints were identified:

  • Human - parking operators
  • Physical - electric car, charging cables, RFID charging card, charging stations
  • Space - manual and automatic parking garages, on-street charging stations
  • Digital - mobile app

The needs, requirements and usage contexts of the user while interacting with these touchpoints were analysed to ensure that a seamless interconnected experience between the different touchpoint typologies.

For the mobile app touchpoint, the features and the respective screens were initially designed by making rough sketches of key screens of the app. This allowed us to quickly iterate and optimise an initial version of information architecture and user flow.

This was followed by making consolidated wireframes digitally of the entire mobile app. Particular attention was given to the flow in-between the user interaction in the mobile app and physical context. For e.g. we gave step by step instructions that assists users on how to efficiently interact with the physical infrastructure to start and end their charging session. And blinking of the booked charging station when users smartphone is in a 5m range for easy identification and easy unlocking of the hatch of the charging station with their smartphone or RFID card.

Wireframes of the entire app were mapped in a flow for various user scenarios. Discussion with stakeholders and the client then allowed us to consolidate the screens and proceed to making the visual design and development of the app.

User validation & mobile usability testing

Once the new Ready to Park & Charge app was developed, we planned for testing and validation with users and parking operators. The goal was to identify breaking points of the experience, why they were so and define insights to fix them.

To organise our research test cards were made. It was important for us to understand why we are doing this test? what do we really want to know/learn from this test? what action could we take if we knew that number or rate?

To be tested was the overall experience, with focus on a set of key moments (booking phase, driving to facility, arrival and car handover, charging of car, payment) which will be validated based on previous assumptions. Certain KPIs were defined so that we could measure metrics, analyse & learn from it. We defined behaviours to observe and the key gains, pains and jobs we wanted to test. Different research methods and tools were used:

  1. Observing participants using the service
  2. Interviewing users and parking operators
  3. Evaluating user’s mobile phone usage through a mobile screen & voice recording app.
  4. Questionnaires for users & parking operators to rate the key steps of the experience.

The testing was carried out in the city of Rome, Italy where 4 scenarios and 3 variables were tested.

Consolidation of app usability based on user testing & feedback

After the testing session, the results and feedback were reviewed to identify the critical problem areas that had to be addressed. insights were drawn and improvement were made to the user experience of the mobile app and the service.

EQ Charge mobile app - Visual design

The EQ Charge service functions in a similar way to the one of the Ready to Park & Charge. Once the Ready to Park & Charge app was completely designed, the visual language of the EQ service was made. We came up with options for colour scheme, buttons, key interactive elements, micro animations & illustrations that reflected the brand identity of Mercedes-Benz EQ.

A/B Testing and consolidation of the visual language of the app

After the visual design of the app was complete, important elements on key screens were A/B tested to evaluate how the well the users understood and perceived these visual elements. The results allows us to further consolidate the design.

03

outcome

Electric mobility should be easy, fast and convenient and above all, directly available. With the Ready to Park & Charge and Mercedes EQ Charge we have designed two innovative mobility services for the urban mobility eco-system and smart city experiences.

With just a swipe on their smartphone we have made it easier than ever for electric car users to find, book, charge and pay for their parking & charging sessions, guiding them through the whole process.

Studying users needs and the existing urban infrastructure, we designed new functionalities that fit perfectly into the existing service, thus adding value by creating a more holistic service ecosystem for Mercedes-Benz.

The Ready to Park & Charge service and mobile app was designed with the goal of enhancing and creating a holistic experience for new customers and already existing smart owners. With this new “ready to” service, we have redefined what urban mobility means.

The EQ Charge service and mobile app truly reflected the electric mobility of from Mercedes-Benz with its look, feel & emotion. The service offers more flexibility as we have created an ecosystem of intelligent mobility solutions designed to address the various demands of current and future Mercedes-Benz customers.
