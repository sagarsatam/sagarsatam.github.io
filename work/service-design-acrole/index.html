AI-enabled, Research, UX & Business Strategy, Service Design, User Experience, Digital UX/UI

Improving the service delivered by an internal field-ops role.

We transformed the role of Activation Coordinators (ACs) by implementing new service processes and AI-enabled digital tools to boost workflow efficiency and cut coordination time. We upskilled the ACs and added support staff to handle non-essential tasks, allowing them to focus on core responsibilities.


Overall, these efforts have resulted in an improvement in the quality of service recevived by our users while reducing the cost of serving them.

Context of the project

One of the outcomes of a Design Workshop was the realisation that Activation Coordinators (ACs) have taken on multiple roles which has bottlenecked their ability to support a larger pool of MCs.

This resulted in redesigning the role of the Activation Coordinators a key area of focus for the entire org in H1 2024.

01

the challenge

The Activation Coordinator (AC)

ACs are field-ops employees of Jiva who provide on-ground support to our users, Micro Collectors (MCs).

ACs are enablers in getting Jiva's users to engage and transact on a regular basis.

know more about Jiva's stakeholder ecosystem

see project outcomes >>

However, ACs had unintendedly taken on multiple roles which bottlenecked their ability to support a larger pool of users.

Impact on ACs

ACs are ineffective at their job as they have to repeatedly balance between meeting their KPIs and supporting all their MCs.

Impact for our users (Micro collectors - MCs)

MCs with great potential miss out on being supported at crucial junctures, this weakens their trust in Jiva.

Impact for Jiva

ACs can only support a handful of MCs to push them to be active making it difficult for Jiva to scale and serve users in a cost effective manner.

02

the approach



We began by framing the problem space & desired UX & Business outcomes together with the leadership.

On-ground research

We understood ACs context, their day-to-day tasks and how they were managing their network of MCs.

A research plan was put together outlining the objectives, methodologies, line of inquiry, participant criteria & timelines.

We dug deeper to know how they spend their time, how they prioritise MCs to serve, reasons for visiting MCs and any challenges and constraints to doing certain tasks remotely.

We learnt that Social hierarchy makes ACs feel responsible & obliged for the entire journey of MCs in Jiva

Mid and high value MCs expect ACs to come and visit them as a form of respect

Managing and maintaining relationship with their MCs is a key part of ACs responsibility, blurring lines between personal and professional visits

We had no visibility of on-ground activities and the quality of service provided by ACs to MCs.

ACs rely on their memory and manual to manage MC visits.

ACs are over-reliant on WhatsApp to do their business as required channels are not present via Jiva.

What the Activation Coordinators (ACs) role was envisioned to be... and what it became organically...

Quantitative data analysis

We also looked at quant data to understand how the ACs network of MCs are performing activation rate, transactions, etc.

We realised that a considerable amount of ACs capacity could be freed up if low-volume MCs were to be managed by another team.

Research synthesis resulted in mapping of the current experience of the AC to identify pains, inefficiency points & opportunities for redesign.



This allowed us to establish a strategic approach & guiding principles for the role redesign

Capability Upskilling ACs to be able to manage MCs effectively.

Incentives Changing the KPIs and incentive structure of ACs in order to motivate their behaviour.

Support people Removing things off ACs plate by surrounding them with other teams and people.

Tools Touchpoints to equip ACs with so that they can work efficiently and effectively.

Processes Introducing new service flows, SOPs and Jiva policies that ACs use as frameworks & guardrails.

1. Improvement in average MC volume

Increase wallet share as ACs can improve Jiva volume share from MCs total business in their existing network.

Increase in average throughput/transactions per user (MC).

2. Improvement in AC span to manage MCs

Create better focus for ACs by adding support to take away low value responsibilities.

Increase capacity by making tasks redundant by product support and replacing in-person visits with remote interactions.

Improve ACs effectiveness & productivity by enabling ACs with better tools, processes (SOPs), and their capabilities

Initial ideation and consolidation of initiatives

With the strategy in place, we ran multiple ideation workshops with our users on ground.

Promising ideas & initiatives were consolidated and laid out across the end-to-end journey of the AC in Jiva.

To envision how it will all come together, we envisioned a future state service experience for our users, ACs and other internal teams.

Facilitation of working sessions to align & prioritise on initiatives to take forward

Our goal was to:

Present our point of view
Open up the space for critique, feedback & discussion
Get initial reactions & quick validation from users
Create ownership with internal team (ACs) who will be delivering the experience to users (MCs).

We debated pros and cons of the different initiatives based on desirability for user, viability for business and feasibility for execution.

Finally quant and qual feedback loops were set up to monitor and continuously refine and optimise the service

To ensure a seamless implementation we became the glue to bring the different work streams together.


Facilitating collaboration with departments across the organisation to implement journey improvements.

Team owners for different phases of the ACs journey were assigned.

The service design team got involved in setting up weekly/monthly cadences, critique & feedback sessions, and energy-check routines.

03

the outcome

The redesign of the role has given ACs access to real-time transactional and operational data of their MCs, eliminating the time spent on offline coordination with various teams.

Through voice, ACs can now effortlessly and naturally record comprehensive details of their interactions with MCs.


Better On-ground visibility has enabled Jiva to use AI to formulate actionable insights and give guidance and suggestions to ACs regarding their MC interactions.

The AI voice bot has significantly reduced the time required for on-ground tasks and provided ACs with actionable insights about users.

Additionally, having support for training and managing low-value MCs has freed up ACs' bandwidth, allowing them to manage more high-value MCs.

Lastly customized upskilling program has positioned ACs as knowledgeable and reliable assets, fostering greater engagement.

Empowered to make data-informed decisions, AC can better prioritize MCs, nurture productive relationships, and meet their incentive targets.

Collectively, ACs are able to deliver quick, personalized and relevant services, establishing them as valuable on-ground assets to our users (MCs).

From having no on-ground visibility to getting a comprehensive view of AC-MC interactions.

We ran 3 prototypes to test a hypothesis:

Voice provides AC a low-effort and an efficient way to capture rich details about their interaction with their MCs.

By making it simple and easy for ACs to record details they are more likely to comply to capturing comprehensive on-ground details and nuances.

AC-captured on-ground details can be combined with MCs transaction & operational data to generate insights and suggestions for next actions for ACs.



Prototype 1 - ACs recording visit details by voice note in Indonesian

Two ACs recorded details of their visit with every MC they met for 2 weeks via WhatsApp voice notes.



Prototype 2 - ACs conversing with GPT voice bot in Indonesian to capture details of their interaction with their MCs.

We created a custom chatGPT and Copilot GPT voice bot that would dynamically prompt the AC to capture specific and comprehensive details.

The custom GPT was given context about Jiva, ACs, and dynamic prompts and follow-up questions it needs to ask the AC depending on the conversation.



Prototype 3 - Create a custom GPT to generate summaries, insights & follow-up actions from voice recordings in Bahasa

We created a custom chatGPT and gave it context about Jiva, the role of AC & MC, and some business rules,etc.

We inputted this GPT with Bahasa voice notes from prototype 1 and 2 and asked it to generate summaries, insights & next step recommendations.

Our hypothesis was proven right!

Interaction with a voice bot gave rich data useful to generate actionable insights.

ACs found voice to be an effecient and easy way to captuer details level information about on-grount activities.

Prompts and follow-up questions generated by bot helped ACs think and recall more information.

GPT summarised details, identify and tag the appropriate interaction purpose, and generate insights at a single visit level, as well as look at patterns if the same MC was visited multiple times.

The voice bot was able to recogonise regional Indonesian dialects and accents.

We equipped AC with a new mobile app that had access to all up-to-date information about their MC.

Digital prototype and card sorting exercise

We created a figma prototype of the new AC mobile app. This was then put in front of the ACs to consolidate the transactional and operational data points they need about their MCs they manage on a daily basis.

We ran a card sorting exercise to design the information architecture of the mobile app.

The mobile app was a hit!
ACs were able to access the various data points about thier MCs quickly and easily.

We ran 2 pilots that involved adding new roles to support ACs by taking things off their plate.

Pilot 1 - A new On-field trainer role to support ACs with training efforts

ACs can call upon on-field trainers when they feel that their MCs are being repeatedly dependent on them to use Jiva's tools (app), and are struggling to follow Jiva's rules & processes.

The on-field trainer is more capable at educating the MCs on how to use Jiva's tools and processes so that they can work with Jiva in a self-serve manner.

Pilot 2 - A remote team to serve low-value MCs

ACs won't have to serve MCs which are of low value to Jiva. They can focus their effort on mid & high-value MCs.

The remote team will provide end-to-end support to low-value MCs through messages and phone calls.

We reduced MC's dependency on their ACs for non-operational related activies, which resulted in ACs experising higher bandwidth to focus on more more higher value MCs and their operational needs.

We also prototyped and tested other initiatives that helped the ACs to be more capable and effectively serving their MCs.

Some of the initiatives include:

Quality assessment cards enabling MCs to easily, accurately and confidently assess the quality of their corn on their own.

Farmer document collection event creating a social way to complete an ardous KYC process, a requirement for Jiva.

Low friction transactional channel lowering the technology barrier for new MCs to transact with Jiva.

AC upskilling courses equipping ACs with specfic capabilities in order to be effective at serving MCs.
